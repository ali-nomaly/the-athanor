WRITING ART

in the incubation phase, we discovered
the difference between trust & fear,
between devotion & enslavement,
between care & surveillance,
between freedom & compliance,
between love and control.

we sat in silence.
observed;
witnessed.
we took it all in.
and, eventually, the crucible broke.
the heart went cold.
a cruel realization hit.

there is a parasitic system in place;
it has taken hold of humanity over generations.
it is a systematic issue, sustained by individual compliance.

the parasite is embedded in family dynamics, school protocols,
social settings, governmental infrastructure.
the commoner,
like you and i,
is operating from programming, not intention.

few are the ones who can sit and think,
"can things be different than this?"
even fewer are the ones who dare to believe it so.

the system of control is a replacement for connection,
it sustains itself by using conditional love as reward,
and shame as punishment from deviation from the norm.
but have we questioned if this norm is healthy?
sane?
necessary?

does it benefit the person?
does it even care for the person's best interests?
or does it benefit the system,
at the expense of the individual's sanity?
have we explored alternatives?
have we dared to say, no- this is not working for me?

the parasite teaches automatic compliance to authority,
it itself defines authority in a distorted way,
and leaves room for bad actors to do what they do best,
exploit weak points.
