MISSION 4A — FORM THE LLC
Register a single-member LLC in Illinois. Go to Illinois.gov → Business Services → LLC formation. Filing fee: ~$150. Takes 1–2 weeks. Get an EIN (Employer Identification Number) from IRS.gov immediately after — it's free and instant online. Open a separate business checking account (a basic free one at a local credit union works). This entity separates your personal and business finances, unlocks business deductions, and makes you a business owner on paper — which is the starting position for everything that follows.

MISSION 4C — OPEN A SEP IRA (AFTER LLC IS FORMED)
A SEP IRA is a self-employed retirement account that lets you contribute up to 25% of your net self-employment income — up to $66,000/year (vs $7,000 for a Roth IRA). Contributions are tax-deductible, reducing your taxable income immediately. Even at modest business income, this becomes a significant tool. Open it at Fidelity alongside your existing Roth IRA. Once the LLC generates consistent income, maxing the SEP IRA is one of the most powerful legal tax reduction tools available to you.
