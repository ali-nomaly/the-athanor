The Layers — What Goes In

Bottom of the trench to the top of the mound · in order

🌾
Layer 5 — Straw Mulch (top)

3–4 inches across the entire mound surface including all slopes. Goes on after planting. Critical on a mound — bare sloped soil erodes on first heavy rain. One bale covers everything.

3–4 in
🌱
Layer 4 — Excavated Soil + Compost

Your tilled dirt shoveled back, mixed with at least 1 bag of compost per bay equivalent. This is where roots live. More of this goes in the center to build the peak shape. Aim for 8–10 inches above the wood everywhere.

8–10 in
🍂
Layer 3 — Green + Brown Organics

Grass clippings, kitchen scraps (no meat), dried leaves, straw, coffee grounds continuously across the full length. Fills gaps in the wood, introduces microbial life, and compensates for first-year nitrogen draw from the fresh logs. Don't skip or skimp this layer.

4–6 in
🌿
Layer 2 — Branches + Smaller Wood

Pack into every gap between logs along the full 23 ft. Nothing loose or airy — dense is the goal. Smaller wood decomposes faster and starts releasing nutrients in the first season.

3–5 in
🪵
Layer 1 — Logs (bottom of trench)

Your log stack goes in first. Biggest pieces in the trench center (where the peak will be), slightly smaller toward the ends. Lay flat, side by side, densely packed. Soak thoroughly once laid — the logs need to be saturated from day one to begin their moisture-reservoir function.

12–18 in trench
04

The Build — Step by Step

One morning dig · one afternoon shape and plant

!

Remove the tires — before anything else

Both photos show tires against the fence inside your build zone. These must come out before you plant food. Local tire shops accept them free or for a small fee. Non-negotiable for a food garden.

1

Run the string line

Measure 4 feet out from the fence along the full 23 ft and run a string or lay a hose. This is your front edge. Everything inside the string is your build zone. Stake both ends.

2

Tiller first, then shovels

Run the tiller across the whole 4×23 ft footprint to loosen the soil. Then the team shovels the loose earth out of the trench — 12 to 18 inches deep — piling all excavated soil on the lawn side of the string. You'll use every bit of it back.

3

Roll in the logs — big ones first, center first

Lay your biggest logs in the center of the trench where the peak will be, slightly smaller toward the ends. Pack tightly side by side. Soak with hose thoroughly before moving on. Wet logs are activated logs.

4

Pack branches and organic fill

Fill every gap with branches and twigs. Then spread grass clippings, kitchen scraps, and dried leaves continuously across the full length. Be generous here — this is the nitrogen buffer that protects your Year 1 planting from wood-draw.

5

Shovel soil back — build the shape as you go

Mix your excavated soil with compost and shovel it back over the whole structure. Here's where the mound gets made: pile more soil in the center over the biggest logs, taper toward the ends, and slope gently from fence to front edge. Rough in the shape first, then refine with a rake. Step back and look at the silhouette often.

6

Lay the brick edge

Set your existing bricks along the front base of the mound before planting. Single row is enough. This defines the garden boundary, keeps soil from washing onto lawn, and makes the whole thing look finished and intentional from day one.

7

Plant, then mulch

Plants go in before straw. Start with transplants (tomatoes, peppers), then seeds (carrot, marigold, sunflower, zinnia, cilantro), then bulbs (onion sets, garlic). Once everything is in the ground, lay 3–4 inches of straw across the entire mound surface including slopes. Water everything deeply.
