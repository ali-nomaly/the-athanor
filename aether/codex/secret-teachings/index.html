Manly P. Hall
1928
I
The Ancient Mysteries & Secret Societies
The origin and purpose of the mystery schools — keepers of a secret tradition stretching from Egypt to Freemasonry.
→ II
The Gnostic Mysteries
The Demiurge, the Pleroma, the divine spark imprisoned in matter — Gnostic cosmology as a map of the soul's descent and return.
→ III
The Eleusinian & Bacchic Mysteries
Persephone's descent, the grain that dies to be reborn — the Greek mysteries of death, initiation, and resurrection.
→ IV
Atlantis & the Gods of Antiquity
Hall's reading of Plato's Atlantis as encoded history — the sunken civilization as source of the mystery tradition.
→ V
Thoth Hermes Trismegistus
The thrice-great scribe of the gods — Egyptian and Greek synthesis, the Hermetic Corpus, the Emerald Tablet.
→ VI
The Initiation of the Pyramid
The Great Pyramid as initiatic temple — geometry, proportion, and the candidate's symbolic death and rebirth within stone.
→ VII
Isis, the Virgin of the World
The veiled goddess — Isis as universal nature, as wisdom herself, as the mother of all mystery.
→ VIII
The Sun, the Light of the World
Solar theology across civilizations — the sun as supreme deity, as symbol of the illumined mind, as cosmic Christ.
