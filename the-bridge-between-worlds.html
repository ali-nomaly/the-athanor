// ADVISORY // TELEOLOGY_PROTOCOL

On the Purpose of the Practice

Standard advisories speak of contraindication — what is dangerous, what to avoid. This frame is insufficient. Following Adler: behavior is teleological. Every practice is in service of an underlying goal, often unconscious.

The question is therefore not "is this technique safe?" but "what am I attempting to accomplish with it?"

Aggressive hyperventilation practiced compulsively may serve a goal of bypassing feeling — using altered states to avoid present contact. Extreme retention in someone with somatic trauma history may serve a goal of proving mastery over the body — recapitulating the original injury through control rather than metabolism. Even "calming" practices, used compulsively, may serve suppression dressed as regulation.

The technique itself is not the danger. The unexamined goal it serves is.

Before each practice, the diagnostic question: → what is this in service of?
