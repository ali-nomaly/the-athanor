01

A Gentle, Honest Starting Point

No shame. No extremes. Just truth worth knowing.

Your family's food culture — the tortillas, the caldo, the frijoles de la olla, the chiles — is not the problem. Traditional Mexican food is, at its roots, one of the healthiest eating patterns on earth.


What happened to most families over the past few decades is a transition — away from whole, home-cooked food and toward processed convenience foods, sugary drinks, large portions of refined carbs, and less movement. That shift — not your heritage — is what's driving the weight, the fatigue, and the chronic conditions your family may be experiencing.


The path forward isn't a diet. It's a return — back toward real ingredients, home cooking, and the wisdom already encoded in your culinary tradition, enriched by what Asian food cultures have long understood about longevity.


Research shows that people who return to traditional Mexican dietary patterns see improvements in cholesterol, blood sugar, inflammation, and body weight — without abandoning the foods they love.

🧬

Overweight ≠ Lazy or Broken

Chronic weight gain is almost always driven by the food environment, processed food design, stress, and inflammation — not a failure of willpower. The body is responding rationally to bad inputs.

🌱

Change Doesn't Require Perfection

Studies show that even a 5–10% reduction in body weight meaningfully improves blood pressure, blood sugar, and joint pain. Small, consistent shifts compound powerfully over time.

🫘

Your Heritage Food Is Medicine

Beans, corn, nopales, chiles, tomatoes, and avocado are among the most researched health-protective foods on the planet. The problem is rarely what's traditional — it's what's been added to it.

🍜

Asian Food Aligns Beautifully

Japanese, Korean, and Chinese food traditions share the same core wisdom: vegetables, fermented foods, seafood, minimal processing, smaller portions, and eating with intention. These are natural bridges to your own values.

❤️

Self-Care Is Not Selfish

Feeding your family well is an act of love and sovereignty. When the body is nourished — not just filled — energy returns, mood lifts, and the whole family feels the difference.

02

The Real Culprit — Processed vs. Whole

This is the single most important distinction in food

⚠️

What's Actually Causing the Damage

Sugary drinks — sodas, juices, aguas frescas with added sugar, sports drinks. One soda = 10 teaspoons of sugar hitting your bloodstream at once.
Ultra-processed flour products — white bread, flour tortillas (commercial), donuts, packaged pastries. Stripped of fiber, they spike blood sugar rapidly.
Fried fast food oils — vegetable/soybean oils heated at high temps create inflammatory compounds. Chicharrón, fried chicken, French fries in commercial oil are different from home-cooked foods.
Packaged snacks — chips, crackers, cookies with long ingredient lists. Engineered to override your body's "I'm full" signal.
Excess refined salt + MSG in processed foods — different from using salt in home cooking. Processed food sodium is in everything and drives hypertension.
Eating without awareness — large portions while distracted (TV, phone), skipping meals then overeating, eating fast. These behaviors override the body's natural fullness signals.
✅

What Actually Heals the Body

Whole grains with their fiber intact — corn tortillas (not flour), brown rice, oats, whole wheat. Fiber slows digestion, feeds gut bacteria, prevents blood sugar spikes.
Beans and legumes, daily — research consistently shows beans are one of the most powerful foods for weight management, blood sugar, and heart health. Eat them freely.
Vegetables at every meal — nopales, chiles, squash, bok choy, spinach, cabbage, broccoli. Volume, fiber, and micronutrients without excess calories.
Fish and seafood 2–3×/week — omega-3 fatty acids directly reduce inflammation, protect the heart and brain. Canned sardines and salmon count.
Fermented foods — salsa made fresh, kimchi, miso, plain yogurt, kefir. Feed the gut microbiome, which controls inflammation and immune function.
Water, tea, and agua de Jamaica (unsweetened). Staying hydrated reduces hunger, supports kidney function, and replaces the empty calories of sweet drinks.
"People lost twice as much body weight eating minimally processed foods compared to ultra-processed foods — even when the ultra-processed version was considered 'healthy.' The food's structure matters more than its label." — Harvard Medical School, 2025

Build Your Plate — La Regla del Plato

A simple visual guide for every meal, every day

½ Plate — Non-Starchy Vegetables

Nopales, calabaza, chiles, bok choy, spinach, broccoli, cabbage, tomatoes, onions, mushrooms, zucchini. Cook or raw. The more variety and color, the better.

¼ Plate — Quality Protein

Prioritize: beans, lentils, fish, tofu, eggs, chicken (grilled or baked). Limit: fatty fried meats, processed meats. Beans can be your protein daily — they're ideal.

¼ Plate — Whole Grains or Starchy Veg

Corn tortillas (2–3), brown rice, oats, sweet potato, quinoa. Not white bread, flour tortillas, or white rice as a base. The grain should earn its plate space.

On the Side — Healthy Fat

¼ avocado, drizzle of olive oil, small handful of nuts or seeds. Fat is not the enemy — it supports satiety, hormone production, and absorption of fat-soluble vitamins.

The Mexican Plate already does this naturally when made from scratch: caldo de pollo with vegetables, beans on the side, 2 corn tortillas, a little salsa verde. That is the template. The problem is the soda next to it, the refried beans cooked in lard, and the flour tortillas the size of a plate.

Gentle Swaps — No Deprivation

Keep the flavors you love. Change what's hurting you.

❌ Replace This → ✅ With This
Soda, Jarritos, sweet juice→Agua de Jamaica (no sugar added), sparkling water + lime, green tea
Large flour tortilla (burrito-size)→2–3 small corn tortillas (nixtamalized masa) — more fiber, less refined carbs
Refried beans fried in lard→Frijoles de la olla (whole beans, slow-cooked in water with garlic + epazote)
White rice as a base with no veg→Brown rice with sautéed bok choy or zucchini, or cauliflower rice mixed half-and-half
Fried chicharrón, carnitas daily→Grilled chicken, baked fish taco, or bean taco as the everyday option — carnitas on occasions
Commercial packaged chips and cookies→Jicama or cucumber with tajín + lime, edamame, roasted pepitas, or fresh fruit
Store-bought taco seasoning packets→Fresh cumin, chili powder, garlic, oregano, salt — make your own blend in 30 seconds
Fast food or take-out as default meal→Batch-cooked beans + rice + salsa + eggs at home in 15 minutes — faster than delivery
Full-sugar coffee drinks (café de olla excess sugar)→Café de olla with just a small amount of piloncillo + cinnamon, or green tea
Deep-fried anything daily→Baked, steamed, grilled, or sautéed in a small amount of olive or avocado oil
Eating from large shared pots / serving bowls→Plate individual portions before eating — the eye calibrates better than the hand
Ramen (instant packaged)→Homemade sopa de fideos or miso soup with tofu + bok choy — same comfort, real food
Ice cream, paletas, sweet desserts daily→Fresh fruit with tajín, Greek yogurt with honey, or homemade agua fresca popsicle
The goal is not perfection. Follow this 80–90% of the time. The remaining 10–20% is for celebrations, family gatherings, and the joy of food. Deprivation creates rebound. Consistency creates change.

Inflammation — The Hidden Driver

Chronic inflammation drives obesity, fatigue, joint pain, and most modern disease

🔥 Foods That Inflame — Limit These
🥤
Sugary drinks — spike insulin, trigger inflammatory cytokines with every sip
🍟
Industrial seed oils (soybean, canola, corn oil) — especially when heated to high temps
🍞
Refined white flour products — strips the fiber that slows glucose absorption
🥩
Processed meats (chorizo, hot dogs, packaged deli) — contain nitrates and excess sodium
🍬
Added sugars and high-fructose corn syrup — directly feeds inflammatory pathways
🧂
Excess sodium from processed foods — raises blood pressure, stresses kidney and heart
😴
Poor sleep, chronic stress, and sedentary living — increase inflammatory markers even when diet is good
❄️ Foods That Cool Inflammation — Eat More of These
🐟
Fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel) — omega-3s directly block inflammatory pathways
🌰
Walnuts, almonds, chia seeds, flaxseed — plant-based omega-3 and polyphenols
🫙
Fermented foods (kimchi, miso, fresh salsa, yogurt) — microbiome diversity = lower inflammation
🫐
Berries, dark cherries, hibiscus — anthocyanins are among the most potent anti-inflammatory compounds
🟡
Turmeric + black pepper — curcumin reduces inflammatory markers comparably to ibuprofen in studies
🫚
Ginger (fresh or dried) — anti-inflammatory and digestive support; use daily in teas, soups
🥦
Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cabbage, nopales) — sulforaphane activates the body's own antioxidant pathways
🍵
Green tea / hibiscus / agua de Jamaica (unsweetened) — among the highest polyphenol beverages on earth
Key Insight: Chronic inflammation is like a slow fire burning inside the body. Excess body fat — especially belly fat — acts as inflammatory tissue itself, releasing signals that make inflammation worse. This is why the cycle of weight gain and inflammation feeds itself. Breaking it starts with food — not exercise, not willpower — food first.

What You Drink Matters as Much as What You Eat

Liquid calories are invisible — and devastating to blood sugar

💧

Water — Always First

8–10 glasses/day minimum. Add lime, cucumber, or mint. Drink a glass before every meal — it reduces how much you eat naturally.

🌺

Agua de Jamaica (Hibiscus)

Brew with NO sugar or a tiny amount of piloncillo. Hibiscus is clinically proven to lower blood pressure and reduce inflammation.

🍵

Green Tea / Matcha

Drink 1–2 cups daily. Polyphenols and EGCG are among the most studied anti-inflammatory compounds. Replace one coffee with green tea.

☕

Café de Olla (Light Sugar)

Traditional Mexican cinnamon coffee is fine — in moderation and with minimal piloncillo. Skip the sugar and cream-loaded versions.

🥤

❌ Sodas / Sweet Drinks

One can of soda = 10 tsp sugar + zero nutrition. This is the single most impactful swap your family can make for weight and metabolic health.

🧃

❌ Fruit Juice

Even 100% fruit juice removes all the fiber and delivers concentrated sugar. Eat the whole fruit. Drink water. Don't drink your fruit.

🧋

❌ Flavored Coffees

A sweetened Starbucks or café drink can contain 40–60g of sugar — more than a can of soda. Make café at home.

🥛

Kefir / Plain Yogurt Drink

Plain kefir or a drinkable yogurt with no added sugar delivers probiotics, calcium, and protein. Great as a snack or post-meal drink.

Habits That Change Everything

Beyond the food — how you eat matters as much as what you eat

🍽️ Eating Behaviors

1
Eat slowly. It takes 20 minutes for your stomach to signal fullness to your brain. Eating fast = always overeating before the signal arrives.
2
No screens while eating. Eating while distracted causes 25–30% more consumption. Sit at the table. Together if possible.
3
Plate your food, don't eat from pots. Portioning visually before eating dramatically reduces overeating.
4
Stop at 80% full (Hara Hachi Bu). A Japanese principle from the longest-lived population on earth. Eat until you're satisfied, not stuffed.
5
Don't skip meals. Hunger debt leads to poor choices and overeating at night. Eat real meals at consistent times.
6
Vegetables first. Start your meal with the vegetable portion. You'll eat less of everything else and get more of what nourishes.

🌙 Daily Rhythms for Health

1
Drink water first thing. Before coffee, food, or your phone. One full glass. Rehydrates organs after sleep and kickstarts metabolism.
2
Walk 30 minutes a day. Research is clear: daily walking is among the highest-impact interventions for weight loss, blood sugar, and longevity — even at a gentle pace.
3
Sleep 7–8 hours. Sleep deprivation directly increases ghrelin (hunger hormone), reduces satiety, and promotes fat storage. Sleep is not a luxury — it's metabolism.
4
Cook together. Families who cook together eat better food and smaller portions. It's also one of the most powerful acts of connection and teaching for children.
5
Weekly batch cook on Sunday. Beans, grains, washed vegetables. 2 hours of prep enables 7 days of easy, real meals.
6
Make peace with food. Shame around eating makes it worse, not better. Each meal is a new beginning. Progress — not perfection — is what heals.

Carry These With You

Pin these in your kitchen. Read them when it's hard.

"La comida es medicina."

Food is medicine — and it has been in your family's kitchen all along. Beans, chiles, nopales, avocado, cilantro — these are healers. You already know how to cook them. Trust that knowledge again.

The goal is not a number on a scale.

The goal is energy to play with your kids and grandkids. The goal is knees that don't ache. The goal is sleeping through the night. The goal is showing your children what caring for a body looks like. The scale follows from all of that.

One swap at a time.

Don't change everything at once. This week: replace one soda per day with water or Jamaica. That's it. One swap, sustained for a month, is more powerful than a dramatic two-week diet that collapses. Progress compounds.

Your children are watching.

86% of parents who changed to whole food eating passed those habits directly to their children. The most powerful nutrition intervention for your kids is not what you tell them — it's what you put on the table and eat yourself.

Self-neglect is learned. So is self-care.

If you grew up seeing adults dismiss their own needs, it takes real intention to do differently. Eating well, sleeping, moving — these are not indulgences. They are the foundation on which everything else in your life rests.

This is a long game.

Sustainable weight loss is 0.5 to 1 lb/week. That's 25–50 lbs in a year without starvation. Inflammation takes 3–6 months to cool. The body heals slowly and permanently when treated with real food and consistency. Trust the process.
