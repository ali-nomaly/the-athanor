Food Preservation Methods

Turn harvest abundance into year-round food security

🫙

Water Bath Canning

SHELF LIFE: 1–2 YEARS
For high-acid foods only (pH ≤ 4.6). Submerge sealed jars in boiling water. Kills microorganisms and creates vacuum seal.
TomatoesFruit Jams PicklesApple Sauce SalsaFruit Preserves
⚗️

Pressure Canning

SHELF LIFE: 2–5 YEARS
Required for low-acid foods. Reaches 240°F under pressure, killing botulism spores. Always use tested USDA recipes.
Meat & FishBeans VegetablesSoups Broths
❄️

Freezing

SHELF LIFE: 8–12 MONTHS
Easiest method, best for preserving texture and nutrients. Blanch vegetables first. Vacuum seal to prevent freezer burn.
BerriesVegetables MeatBread MealsHerbs
🌞

Dehydrating

SHELF LIFE: 3–5 YEARS
Removes moisture to stop microbial growth. Lightweight and compact storage. Retains most nutrients.
Fruit LeatherJerky HerbsMushrooms TomatoesApples
🫧

Lacto-Fermentation

SHELF LIFE: 6–18 MONTHS
Beneficial bacteria (probiotics) naturally preserve food and enhance nutrition. No special equipment needed — just salt and time.
SauerkrautKimchi PicklesKefir KombuchaMiso
🏚️

Root Cellaring

SHELF LIFE: 3–8 MONTHS
No electricity needed. Cool (32–50°F), dark, slightly humid space. Can use a basement corner, unheated garage, or buried crate.
PotatoesCarrots OnionsWinter Squash ApplesBeets

1

FIFO — First In, First Out

Always place new stock behind old. Consume oldest items first. Label everything with purchase or canning date. Check inventory every 6 months.

2

The Enemy Trinity: Heat · Light · Moisture

Store in cool (below 70°F), dark spaces. Avoid above the stove or against exterior walls. Use airtight containers — mylar bags + oxygen absorbers for bulk grains.

3

Stock What You Actually Eat

A pantry of foods your family won't eat is wasted space. Build around 10–15 core meals your family loves, then stock 3–4 months of those ingredients.

4

Inventory System

Keep a running list (paper or app) on the pantry door. Note quantity and date. Rotate weekly. Do a full inventory audit every 3 months to identify gaps before they become emergencies.

5

Pest-Proof Storage

Transfer bulk goods to glass jars or food-grade 5-gallon buckets with gamma-seal lids. Never store directly on concrete floor. Check regularly for signs of moisture or infestation.

6

Build in Tiers

Start with 2-week supply → expand to 1 month → then 3 months → then 6–12 months. Add 1–2 extra items per grocery run. Steady progress beats panic-buying.

Rotation, Inventory & Water Storage

Systems that keep your pantry alive and prevent waste

The FIFO Rotation System
🛒

Purchase / Harvest

Bring in new stock from store, garden, or CSA. Label immediately with date.

↓
🔄

Stock to the Back

Push existing stock forward. New items go behind. Never mix dates in one container.

↓
📝

Log the Inventory

Update your list (whiteboard, app, or notebook). Note quantity, date added, expiry.

↓
🍽️

Cook From the Front

Always use the oldest item first. Oldest = front of shelf = first to be used.

↓
🔁

Audit Every 3 Months

Identify gaps, nearing-expiry items, and rotation failures. Restock accordingly.

💧 Water Storage — The Most Critical Prep

Humans need 1 gallon per person per day (2 gallons in heat or for nursing). Water becomes critical in 3 days. Store before food.

🫙
Minimum: 3-day supply (3 gal/person). Start here.
🪣
Target: 2-week supply (14 gal/person) in sealed food-grade containers.
🛢️
Advanced: 55-gallon water barrels + hand pump for 30-day+ reserves.
🔽
Filtration: Berkey filter or Sawyer Squeeze as backup for any water source.
🔄
Rotate: Tap water in sealed containers lasts 6–12 months. Label date and replace annually.
☀️
Solar Disinfection: Clear bottles + 6 hrs of direct sunlight = SODIS purification method.

Year-Round Indoor Growing

Fresh food without outdoor space — herbs, sprouts, microgreens & more

🌿 Window Herb Garden

South-facing window gets 6+ hours of sun. Use a 6-inch pot per herb. Most herbs thrive in well-draining soil and require minimal care.

Basil Chives Parsley Thyme Oregano Mint (own pot) Rosemary

🌱 Sprouts — Fastest Food

Ready in 3–7 days. No soil, no light required. Just a mason jar and cheesecloth. Nutritionally dense — up to 40x more nutrients than the adult plant.

Broccoli Sprouts Alfalfa Lentil Sprouts Mung Beans Sunflower Radish

🌿 Microgreens

Harvest in 7–14 days. Grown in shallow trays with 1–2 inches of soil. Need moderate light (grow light or bright south window). 200% nutrient density vs. mature plant.

Sunflower Pea Shoots Radish Arugula Kale Beets

🥬 Container Greens

Lettuce, spinach, and kale thrive in 8–12 inch deep containers. Use a grow light (16 hrs/day) in low-light rooms. Succession-sow every 3 weeks for continuous harvest.

Butter Lettuce Spinach Arugula Bok Choy Baby Kale

🍅 Compact Fruiting Plants

Dwarf tomatoes, peppers, and cucumbers can produce indoors with a quality grow light (full-spectrum LED, 12–16 hrs). Pollinate flowers manually with a small brush.

Tumbling Tom Tomato Mini Peppers Bush Cucumber Dwarf Basil

🍄 Mushroom Kits

All-in-one inoculated grow bags produce multiple flushes over 2–3 months. Keep in a cool, humid spot (65–75°F). Spray with water twice daily. No soil needed.

Oyster Mushroom Shiitake Lion's Mane King Oyster
Light Tip: A single full-spectrum grow light (45–65W LED, 2700–6500K) placed 6–12 inches above plants for 14–16 hours/day will support any indoor food crop. Cost: ~$30–80. Return on investment in 1–2 harvests of microgreens or herbs.
